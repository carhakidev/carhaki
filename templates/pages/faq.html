About the Reports

What is a CarHaki vehicle history report?
A CarHaki US Vehicle Report summarises the recorded history of a USA-sourced Tokunbo car based on official US government and insurance databases. It includes title records (salvage, rebuilt, flood), accident history, odometer readings, open safety recalls, theft records, and an AI-generated plain-English verdict.
How is CarHaki different from trusting the seller?
A seller can tell you anything about a car. CarHaki accesses official US government databases (NMVTIS, NHTSA) that record what actually happened — regardless of what the seller claims. If a car was declared a total loss, had its mileage rolled back, or is under a safety recall, those facts cannot be altered.
Can a report guarantee a car is in good condition?
No report can guarantee perfect mechanical condition. CarHaki shows the recorded history, but not all damage is reported to databases. We always recommend an independent mechanical inspection in addition to a CarHaki report.
What does a salvage title mean for a Tokunbo car in Nigeria?
A salvage title means a US insurance company declared the vehicle a total loss — typically after a serious accident, flood, or theft. Salvage-titled cars are cheaper to buy but may have structural weaknesses affecting safety and resale value. Nigerian banks typically do not finance salvage-titled vehicles.

Searching for a Vehicle

What is a VIN and where do I find it on the car?
A VIN (Vehicle Identification Number) is a 17-character alphanumeric code that uniquely identifies every USA-sourced vehicle. Find it on the metal plate visible through the windscreen on the driver's side dashboard, on the driver's door jamb sticker, or on the seller's import documents. VINs never include the letters I, O, or Q.
I searched but got no results. What does this mean?
No results can mean the VIN was incorrectly transcribed, the vehicle predates electronic records, or no events have been reported to NMVTIS for that VIN. No results do not mean the car has a bad history. Contact us at support@carhaki.com if you need help.
Does CarHaki only support USA-sourced Tokunbo cars?
Currently CarHaki supports vehicles imported from the United States using the 17-character VIN. These account for the majority of Tokunbo vehicles entering Nigeria through Cotonou, Apapa, and Tin Can Island ports. USA VINs give access to the most comprehensive government vehicle databases in the world.

Payments

What payment methods does CarHaki accept?
CarHaki accepts card payments (Visa/Mastercard), bank transfer, and USSD via Paystack. All prices are in Nigerian Naira (NGN). A single US Vehicle Report costs ₦15,000.
I paid but did not receive my report. What should I do?
Reports are usually ready within 30 seconds of payment. If more than 10 minutes have passed and your report is not in your dashboard, contact us at support@carhaki.com with your Paystack transaction reference. We will investigate and either deliver your report or process a full refund.
Are reports refundable?
Once a report has been successfully generated, it is non-refundable because data retrieval costs have been incurred. If generation completely failed after payment was confirmed, we will regenerate it or issue a full refund. Contact support@carhaki.com within 7 days with your transaction reference.

Yes. A 3-report bundle costs ₦35,000 (save ₦10,000 vs buying separately) and a 5-report bundle costs ₦50,000 (save ₦25,000). See our Pricing page for full details.

Understanding Results

What do the letter grades A through F mean?
CarHaki uses a penalty scoring system starting at 100 points. Points are deducted for each negative finding: salvage title (−40), flood damage (−35), not actual mileage (−30), rebuilt title (−20), major accident (−20), theft (−20), open recall (−10 each), minor accident (−8). Final score: A=90–100, B=75–89, C=60–74, D=45–59, E=20–44, F=0–19.
The report shows the car was sold as salvage. Should I still buy it?
A salvage record is serious but not an automatic disqualification. Key questions: Was it repaired and does it now have a rebuilt title? What was the nature of the damage? CarHaki always recommends a thorough independent mechanical inspection for any salvage-history vehicle.
The mileage does not match what the seller told me. Is this fraud?
A discrepancy between recorded mileage and the seller's stated figure is a serious red flag that may indicate odometer tampering, which is illegal in the USA. CarHaki flags odometer discrepancies clearly. We strongly advise against purchasing any vehicle with suspected odometer fraud.
There are open recalls on the vehicle. What should I do?
An open recall means a safety defect has been identified by the manufacturer and not yet repaired. For a vehicle already imported to Nigeria, contact the manufacturer's regional office to enquire about local remedy options, or have a qualified mechanic assess the severity. Read the recall summary carefully before proceeding.
